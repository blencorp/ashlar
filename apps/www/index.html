What it does

Three things federal teams cannot get from a component library alone.

Audit

ashlar audit enforces Federal Web Standards, USWDS conventions, and component anti-patterns in CI. SARIF output lands in GitHub Code Scanning, GitLab, and Azure DevOps.

First-party language coverage: HTML, TSX, JSX, CSS. Vue, Svelte, Astro, ERB by opt-in.

Sign and verify

Components ship as content-addressed, Sigstore-signed capsules. The lockfile records hashes; ashlar verify validates signatures against the embedded chain.

Air-gapped agencies mirror the registry with a bundled trust root.

Update safely

Source ownership with three-way merge. Local customizations survive upstream updates; codemods carry breaking changes; accessibility-critical files force confirmation.

The shadcn-killer differentiator, made safe.

Standards alignment

Engineered to WCAG 2.2 AA. Honest about every legal baseline.

  • Section 508 — incorporates WCAG 2.0 AA. Ashlar engineers to WCAG 2.2 AA; component-level evidence does not certify application-level compliance.
  • ADA Title II — references WCAG 2.1 AA. Compliance dates April 26, 2027 (50,000+ pop.) and April 26, 2028 (smaller and special-district governments).
  • Federal Web Standards — banner, page title, meta description (pending); contact (draft); content timeliness, search (research). Ashlar's federal policy pack tracks each standard's status and emits findings with that status visible in --explain and SARIF output.
  • America by Design (Aug 2025 EO) — initial results required by July 4, 2026. Ashlar is positioned as a substrate the Chief Design Officer's office can cite without building.

USWDS posture

Informed by USWDS. Designed to interoperate.

USWDS markup passes Ashlar's federal policy audit. USWDS components can ship as Ashlar capsules. Where USWDS provides components, Ashlar adds enforcement, evidence, and updates. Ashlar is not the next USWDS — it is the substrate USWDS could run on.
