Why teams move to Ashlar

The vocabulary government already trusts, without the parts that slow you down.

The federal stack today

USWDS gave us a vocabulary, then left teams holding the bag.

  • A vendored stylesheet you can't audit, can't extend, can't sign.
  • Accessibility evidence buried in someone's sprint review notes.
  • Drupal and React forks that drift the second nobody's looking.
  • No way to prove what's on disk matches what was published.
With Ashlar

Source you own, with the receipts a reviewer will accept.

  • Components copied into your repo as real source. .tsx · .twig · .css · diff in PRs
  • Accessibility evidence travels with every component. axe results · keyboard map · SR transcript
  • One spec renders to React and Twig. No fork. single source · shared tokens
  • Sigstore-signed releases, hashes you can verify on disk. ashlar verify ✓
Install · sign · verify

One command. Audit ready by default.

~/projects/benefits-portal · ashlar signed
What government design systems should be
